# Day 2: Reactivate Past Clients

## Win Back the Customers Who Already Know You're Worth It

Past customers are your highest-probability revenue source when you're in a sales drought. They've already made the hardest decision — trusting you enough to pay you. They know your work. They know your value. All they need is a reason to come back.

Today, you're going to give them that reason.

---

## Why Past Customers Convert at 3–5x the Rate of New Leads

Selling to a new prospect requires building trust from scratch. They don't know you, they're skeptical, and they need to be convinced. That takes time, energy, and often several touchpoints before a sale happens.

Selling to a past customer requires none of that groundwork. They already trust you. The only question is: do they need your help again?

Marketing research consistently shows that returning customer conversion rates are 3–5x higher than new customer acquisition rates. If you have even 10–20 past clients, you have a serious asset you're probably underusing.

---

## Step 1: Build Your Past Customer List

Open your invoices, payment records, email threads, or DMs. Write down every person who has ever paid you for anything — regardless of when, how much, or whether the experience was perfect.

Then tag each one:
- **Hot:** Bought from you in the last 6 months, positive experience
- **Warm:** Bought from you 6–24 months ago
- **Cold:** Bought more than 2 years ago, or experience was mixed

Start with Hot, then Warm. Cold can be activated later.

---

## Step 2: The "I've Been Thinking About You" Approach

The most effective past customer reactivation message doesn't feel like a sales email. It feels like a personal check-in from someone who genuinely remembered them.

This works because it's true. You're not making up a reason to reach out — you're acknowledging a real past relationship and offering something that could help them.

**Email Template:**
> Subject: [Name] — thought of you
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I was [going through some old projects / thinking about great clients I've worked with] and you came to mind.
>
> How have things been going with [THING YOU HELPED THEM WITH OR THEIR BUSINESS]?
>
> I'm reaching out because [GENUINE REASON — you have availability, you've added something new, you have an offer this week]. I wanted to offer it to past clients first before opening it up broadly.
>
> [ONE SENTENCE describing the offer or new work you're doing]
>
> Would this be useful for you right now? Happy to hop on a quick call if so.
>
> [Your Name]

**Text/DM Template:**
> Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name]. I was thinking about you the other day — how's everything going with [BUSINESS/PROJECT]? I've been [BRIEF UPDATE] and just wanted to reconnect. Do you have 10 minutes this week for a quick catch-up?

---

## Step 3: The Comeback Offer

For past customers who respond positively, have a specific comeback offer ready. Don't make them ask "what exactly are you offering?" — have it ready.

The best comeback offers:
- Reference what they bought before: "Based on the [WORK WE DID], I think you'd be ready for [NEXT STEP]"
- Offer a returning-customer discount or bonus: "As a returning client, I'll [DISCOUNT/BONUS]"
- Have a clear time component: "I have [NUMBER] spots open this week"

**Example comeback offers by industry:**
- Designer: "I have 2 rush project slots open this week. As a returning client, I can prioritize you."
- Coach: "I'm running a 4-week intensive next month. Past clients get early access at the rate from last year."
- Service business: "We have availability this Thursday and Friday. Returning customers get 15% off their first service back."
- Freelancer: "I'm taking on 2 new retainer clients this month. Based on what we built together, I think [SPECIFIC PROJECT] would make sense as a next step."

---

## Step 4: Handle the "Not Right Now" Gracefully

Some past clients will respond but not be ready to buy. That's fine — the goal of today isn't just to generate immediate sales, it's to warm up your pipeline. A person who says "not right now, but maybe in [month]" is a warm lead for your follow-up sequence in Day 7.

**Response for not-right-now:**
> "Totally understand — no pressure at all. I'll check back in around [DATE]. In the meantime, if anything comes up where I might be helpful, don't hesitate to reach out."

Then put them in your Day 7 follow-up list.

## Day 2 Checklist

- [ ] Sent personal reactivation messages to all Hot customers
- [ ] Sent messages to top 5–10 Warm customers
- [ ] Prepared a comeback offer ready to share when someone responds
- [ ] Added "not right now" responses to Day 7 follow-up list

---

**Target for today:** Reach out to at least 10–15 past customers. If you have fewer than 10, send to all of them and supplement with the Day 1 network list.

Even a 15% response rate on 10 past customers means 1–2 warm conversations that could close this week.
