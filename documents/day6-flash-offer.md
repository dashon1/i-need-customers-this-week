# Day 6: The 48-Hour Flash Offer

## Create a Limited-Time Offer That Gives People a Real Reason to Buy Now

By Day 6, you've warmed your network, reactivated past clients, asked for referrals, built local visibility, and posted social proof. Now it's time to create urgency — a real, time-limited reason for people to act today instead of "someday."

The flash offer is your most direct same-week revenue driver.

---

## What Makes a Flash Offer Work

A flash offer has three components that all have to be present:

**1. A compelling value proposition**
The offer has to be worth acting on. A 5% discount is not compelling. A meaningful discount, a bonus, a bundled value, or limited access is compelling.

**2. A real, hard deadline**
The deadline has to be genuine. "48 hours" that turns into a permanent discount trains people to wait. But a real deadline — Monday by midnight, then it's gone — creates real urgency.

**3. A specific, clear call to action**
"Interested? Let me know!" is not a CTA. "Reply to this email with 'YES' and I'll send you the invoice" or "Click here to lock it in before midnight" is a CTA.

---

## Step 1: Design Your Flash Offer

Answer these questions:

**What are you offering?**
Pick one thing — a service, a product, a bundle. Don't make it complicated.

**What's the offer?**
Options:
- Discount: 20–30% off a specific service or product (today and tomorrow only)
- Bundle: Two things together for less than they'd cost separately
- Bonus: Buy your core service and get a specific add-on free
- Early access: Access to something new before it's widely available
- Limited seats: "I'm only taking 3 new clients this month at this rate"

**What's the deadline?**
Name it precisely: "This offer expires Wednesday at midnight" not "for a limited time."

**What's the CTA?**
What exactly do you want people to do? Call, email, click a link, reply to a text?

---

## Step 2: Price It Right

Pricing your flash offer:
- The discount should be meaningful enough to motivate action but not so deep it destroys your margin or positions you as cheap
- 20–30% off is a sweet spot for service businesses
- Bundle pricing should make the combined value feel obvious: "$200 separately, $149 together"
- If you don't want to discount, offer a meaningful bonus instead of dropping price

---

## Step 3: Announce It Everywhere

Send the announcement through every channel where your audience exists:

**Email announcement:**
> Subject: [NAME], I'm doing something I almost never do — 48-hour offer inside
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> I'm doing a 48-hour offer on [PRODUCT/SERVICE] that I wanted to make sure you knew about.
>
> From now until [DAY] at midnight, you can get [OFFER DETAILS — what's included, what's the price].
>
> Why now? [HONEST REASON — you have availability, celebrating a milestone, helping your best customers, etc.]
>
> This expires at midnight [DATE]. After that, it's gone.
>
> To claim it: [SPECIFIC ACTION — reply YES, click here, call this number]
>
> [Your Name]

**Text announcement:**
> [Name]! 48-hour offer: [OFFER IN ONE SENTENCE]. Expires [DAY] midnight. Reply "YES" to grab it or call [PHONE].

**Social post:**
> Flash offer — [PRODUCT/SERVICE] for [PRICE] instead of [REGULAR PRICE]. Today and tomorrow only.
>
> Here's what you get: [BRIEF LIST]
>
> Expires [DATE/TIME]. [LINK or "DM me to claim"]

---

## Step 4: Mention Why

Offers are more believable when there's a reason behind them. You don't need an elaborate story. Just be honest:
- "I have [X] openings this week"
- "I just wrapped a big project and have bandwidth to take on new work"
- "We're celebrating [milestone]"
- "I'm trying to hit a goal and want to offer something special to make it happen"

People respond to honesty. A business owner saying "I want to fill my calendar this week" is more trustworthy than vague "limited time" language.

---

## Day 6 Checklist

- [ ] Designed the flash offer (what, price, deadline, CTA)
- [ ] Sent email announcement
- [ ] Sent text to relevant contacts
- [ ] Posted on social media
- [ ] Set a reminder to send a final "last call" message before deadline

---

**Don't skip the follow-up.** Send a "last call" message 4–6 hours before your deadline. Something like: "Just a reminder — the [OFFER] expires tonight at midnight. After that, I'm back to regular pricing." This final message often produces as many conversions as the initial announcement.
