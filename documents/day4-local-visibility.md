# Day 4: Local Visibility

## Show Up Where Your Community Is Already Looking

You don't need a big following or a paid ads budget to be visible to local customers. Today you're going to show up in every local digital channel where people in your area are already searching for what you do.

This is especially powerful for service businesses, brick-and-mortar shops, and any business that primarily serves a local area. But even national freelancers can use local channels to land clients nearby.

---

## Why Local Channels Are Underused (And Why That's Good News for You)

Most small business owners are focused on social media follower counts and Instagram aesthetics. Meanwhile, Google Business Profile, Nextdoor, and local Facebook groups are producing steady leads for businesses that simply show up consistently.

These channels have lower competition (most businesses ignore them) and higher buyer intent (people actively searching for a service nearby are much further down the buying funnel than someone passively scrolling Instagram).

---

## Channel 1: Google Business Profile

If you have a local business and you haven't posted on your Google Business Profile in the last 30 days, you're invisible in local search.

Google Business posts appear in Google Search and Google Maps when people search for your type of business nearby. They're free, take 5 minutes, and show the people most likely to buy from you — people actively searching for what you do right now.

**Post type to use today:** "What's New" post

**Template:**
> We have [NUMBER] availability / [PRODUCT IN STOCK] / [OFFERING AVAILABLE] this week. [ONE SENTENCE describing what you do and who you help]. Call us at [PHONE] or visit [WEBSITE] to book/order.

**Tips:**
- Include a photo — posts with photos get 3x more views
- Include a call-to-action button (Book, Call, Order Online)
- Post at least once a week for sustained visibility

---

## Channel 2: Nextdoor

Nextdoor is the most underrated local marketing channel for small businesses. Neighbors actively recommend and ask for business recommendations on Nextdoor, and posts from business owners (done right) get engaged responses.

**How to set up:** Create a free Nextdoor Business page at business.nextdoor.com

**Post approach for today:**
Don't hard-sell. Introduce yourself and offer something useful.

**Template:**
> Hi neighbors! I'm [Name], the owner of [BUSINESS NAME] in [NEIGHBORHOOD/CITY]. We [WHAT YOU DO — one clear sentence].
>
> This week we [HAVE AVAILABILITY / ARE RUNNING A SPECIAL / JUST LAUNCHED SOMETHING]. If you've been looking for [WHAT YOU DO], I'd love to help.
>
> I'm local, I'm reachable, and [BRIEF CREDIBILITY LINE — years in business, number of customers served, specific thing you're known for].
>
> Feel free to message me with any questions or just to say hi!

**Bonus:** Encourage your happy local customers to recommend you on Nextdoor. A trusted neighbor recommendation is worth more than any ad.

---

## Channel 3: Local Facebook Groups

Most cities and towns have multiple active Facebook groups: local buy/sell groups, community groups, neighborhood groups, business owner groups, moms groups, and more. These groups often have thousands of members who are your potential customers.

**Find the right groups:**
- Search Facebook for your city name + "community," "buy sell," "moms of [city]," "small business," etc.
- Join 3–5 relevant groups
- Read the group rules before posting — many groups require you to wait or introduce yourself first

**Post template for community groups:**
> Hi everyone! I'm [NAME], a local [WHAT YOU DO] based in [AREA]. I help [WHO YOU SERVE] with [WHAT PROBLEM YOU SOLVE].
>
> I have [NUMBER] spots available this week. If you or anyone you know is looking for [YOUR SERVICE], I'd love to connect. Feel free to comment or DM me!
>
> [Optional: Brief testimonial or result — "I've helped 50+ local families / businesses / etc."]

**Note:** Some groups don't allow promotional posts. In those cases, comment helpfully on threads where people are asking for recommendations, or just build visibility through helpful comments first.

## Channel 4: Physical Community Board

If your business serves a local physical community — a neighborhood, a building, a school area — a physical flyer on a community board can still generate leads.

Laundromats, coffee shops, libraries, coworking spaces, gyms, church lobbies, and grocery store entrances all often have community bulletin boards.

**What to put on the flyer:**
- Your business name and what you do (one clear sentence)
- A specific offer or availability ("Taking new clients this month")
- Your phone number in large text — the bigger the better
- Optional: tear-off strips at the bottom with your number

**Canva** has free flyer templates you can customize in 10 minutes.
