# Day 7: The Follow-Up Machine

## Follow Up With Everyone Who Didn't Respond — Then Close

You've spent six days reaching out to past customers, asking for referrals, building local visibility, creating social proof, and launching a flash offer. Some of those people responded and converted. But many of them didn't respond — and most of those people are not lost. They're just not ready yet, or they got busy, or your message got buried.

Today you run the follow-up sequence. This is where most of the remaining revenue from your week lives.

---

## The Uncomfortable Truth About Follow-Up

Research across industries consistently shows that:
- 44% of salespeople give up after one follow-up
- 80% of sales require 5+ contacts before a close
- Most conversions happen between contact #3 and #7

Most solopreneurs and small business owners reach out once, hear nothing, and assume the person is not interested. In most cases, they are interested — they're just busy, distracted, or waiting for the right moment.

The follow-up is not being annoying. It is being professional.

---

## Who to Follow Up With Today

Go back through everything you did this week and make a list of everyone who:

1. Received a message from Day 1 (network audit) and didn't respond
2. Received a reactivation email from Day 2 and didn't respond
3. Was asked for a referral on Day 3 and gave you a name but you haven't heard back
4. Saw your social posts or local listings and engaged but didn't reach out
5. Received your flash offer email or text and didn't respond or convert

## The 3-Touchpoint Follow-Up Sequence

### Touchpoint 1: The Simple Bump (Today)

This is not a repeat of your original message. It's a single-line bump that acknowledges you haven't heard from them and gives them a frictionless way to respond.

**Template:**
> Hey [Name], just wanted to bump this back up in case it got buried. No pressure either way — just wanted to make sure you had a chance to see it. Still [OFFER/AVAILABLE/INTERESTED IN CONNECTING]?

**Why it works:** It's non-invasive, acknowledges their busy-ness, and doesn't add pressure. Many people who meant to respond but forgot will respond to this immediately.

---

### Touchpoint 2: New Angle (3–5 Days Later)

If they don't respond to the bump, try a different angle 3–5 days later. Don't repeat the same message — come back with something new: a different offer, a new piece of social proof, or a direct question about whether they're still a fit.

**Template:**
> Hey [Name], just checking back in. I shared [SOMETHING NEW — case study, testimonial, result from another client] this week that I thought you might find relevant given [THEIR SITUATION].
>
> Still thinking about [WHAT YOU DISCUSSED/OFFERED]? Happy to jump on a quick call if you have questions.

---

### Touchpoint 3: The Graceful Exit (7–10 Days After Original)

If they still haven't responded, send a final message that closes the loop politely. This message often gets responses because it creates a small loss aversion — they feel the door closing.

**Template:**
> Hey [Name], I've reached out a couple of times and haven't heard back, so I'll assume the timing isn't right. I'll take you off my follow-up list — no hard feelings at all.
>
> If you ever need [WHAT YOU DO] in the future, I'm here. And feel free to reach back out whenever the timing is better.
>
> [Your Name]

This message works because:
- It respects their time and doesn't guilt-trip
- It creates a clear "last chance" moment without being pushy
- It leaves the door open for them to re-engage when they're ready
- Many people respond to this message because they feel bad about not responding earlier

---

## How to Close on the Phone

When a follow-up produces a response and they're interested, move to the phone as quickly as possible. Phone or video calls convert at 2–3x the rate of email chains.

**Phone close framework:**

1. **Re-qualify:** "To make sure I'm clear on what you're looking for — you mentioned [THEIR SITUATION]. Is that still the case?"

2. **Present:** Give a brief, specific summary of your offer. One minute or less. Lead with the outcome they get.

3. **Ask:** "Does that sound like the right fit for what you're trying to do?"

4. **Handle objections:** Let them voice their concern fully before responding. Then address the specific objection (price, timing, fit) directly.

5. **Close:** "So the next step would be [SPECIFIC NEXT ACTION — I'll send you an invoice, let's book a time, I'll get your info]. Does that work for you?"

## End-of-Week Debrief

You've just run a full 7-day customer generation sprint. Before you move on, take 10 minutes to answer these questions:

1. How many people did you reach out to total across all 7 days?
2. How many responded?
3. How many converted to paying customers or bookings?
4. Which day/tactic produced the most responses?
5. Which tactic will you make a permanent part of your monthly routine?

The goal was never just "survive this week." It was to build a repeatable set of habits that prevent the next drought before it starts.

**Going forward:**
- Run Days 1–3 at the beginning of every month (takes 2–3 hours)
- Run Day 4 weekly (takes 20 minutes)
- Run Day 5 twice per week (review request + one piece of social proof content)
- Keep the follow-up sequence running — anyone who doesn't respond to Touchpoint 1 should receive Touchpoints 2 and 3
