# Day 3: The Referral Blitz

## Ask for Referrals the Right Way

Referrals are the highest-converting lead source for almost every small business and freelancer. A referred prospect already has a baseline of trust — someone they respect vouched for you. Close rates on referrals are typically 3–5x higher than cold leads.

But most business owners never ask. Or they ask badly. Today you're going to ask the right way — with a specific script and a real incentive — and create a wave of warm introductions.

---

## Why Most People Never Ask for Referrals

There are three reasons business owners don't ask for referrals:

1. **They feel awkward.** Asking for a referral feels like admitting the business isn't doing well, or like they're imposing on the relationship.

2. **They ask too vaguely.** "Let me know if anyone comes to mind!" is not an ask — it's a hope. People are busy. A vague ask produces vague results.

3. **They assume happy customers will refer automatically.** Happy customers want to refer you, but they're busy and they forget. A specific ask at the right moment is what turns goodwill into action.

---

## The 3-Question Referral Script

This script works because it's specific, easy to answer, and doesn't put pressure on the person being asked.

**Phone or in-person version:**
> "[Name], I have a quick favor to ask. You've been working with us / bought from us, and I really appreciate it. I'm trying to grow the business and I could use your help.
>
> Do you know anyone — maybe a friend, a colleague, or someone in your industry — who [DESCRIBES YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMER'S SITUATION]?
>
> I'm not asking you to do the selling — just an introduction. I'll take it from there. And if they become a customer, I'll [INCENTIVE] for you.
>
> Anyone come to mind?"

The three questions embedded in this script:
1. Do they know someone?
2. Can they make an introduction?
3. Is there someone specific they're thinking of right now?

When you pause after "Anyone come to mind?" — wait. Don't fill the silence. The pause is where the referral happens.

**Email version:**
> Subject: A quick favor + a thank you
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm reaching out because you're one of my favorite customers/clients and I want to ask a favor.
>
> I'm actively looking to work with [DESCRIPTION OF IDEAL CUSTOMER — be specific: "other restaurant owners in the area," "freelance designers who are trying to get more clients," "women over 40 dealing with skin changes"].
>
> Do you know anyone who fits that description? If you can make an introduction, I'll [INCENTIVE] as a thank you.
>
> Just reply with their name and I'll handle the rest.
>
> Thank you,
> [Your Name]

---

## How to Incentivize Without Being Awkward

The incentive doesn't need to be huge. It needs to be:
- **Proportional to the ask** — don't offer $500 for a referral on a $100 product
- **Something they actually want** — cash/credit toward future services beats branded merchandise
- **Clear and specific** — "I'll give you $25 credit" beats "I'll take care of you"

**Good incentive structures:**
- Service businesses: "I'll give you a free add-on / complimentary visit / $25 credit on your next service"
- Freelancers: "I'll give you 10% of the first invoice as a referral fee"
- Product businesses: "I'll send you a $20 gift card to [THEIR FAVORITE PLACE] when they make their first purchase"
- Coaches / consultants: "I'll give you a free 30-minute strategy call as a thank you"

Note: you don't have to offer cash. Many people refer because they genuinely want to help you — the incentive just signals that you value their effort.

---

## Day 3 Action Plan

**Step 1:** Make a list of your happiest 10–15 customers from the last 90 days. These are the people most likely to say yes.

**Step 2:** Call 3–5 of them. Phone calls convert referral asks at 3–5x the rate of emails. A 2-minute call asking for a referral is one of the highest-ROI activities in your business.

**Step 3:** Email the rest using the template above.

**Step 4:** When someone gives you a name, follow up within 24 hours. The faster you follow up, the warmer the introduction is.
