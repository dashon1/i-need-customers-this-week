Cold DM Scripts

Direct Message Templates
Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook Messenger

1 Compliment + Question DM

"Hey [Name]! I came across your [profile/post/business] and really admired how you [specific thing].

Quick question — have you ever looked into [your service]? I've been helping [target audience] with [outcome] and thought this might be relevant for you.

No pressure at all, but happy to chat if you're curious!"

Works best when you can point to something specific they posted or did.

2 Value First DM

"Hey [Name]! I noticed you mentioned [their challenge] in your recent post about [topic].

I've worked with [similar clients] on this, and one insight that often helps is [specific tip].

Would love to share more if it's useful. Want me to send you a quick breakdown?"

Give before you ask. This builds trust and opens the conversation naturally.

3 Direct but Friendly DM

"Hey [Name] — I help [target audience] with [your service], and after seeing your work, I think there's a good fit.

Worth a 5-minute chat if you're open to it?"

Short and confident. Works for busy professionals who appreciate directness.
Email Templates
For warm leads and cold outreach

4 Problem-Solution Email Email

"Subject: Quick question about [their challenge]

Hi [Name],

I noticed [something specific about them] and wanted to reach out.

I help businesses like yours with [your service], specifically by [specific outcome].

One thing I often see with [their industry/challenge] is [common problem]. If that's relevant, I'd love to share how we've helped others in your space.

Worth a quick 15-minute call this week?

Best,
[Your Name]"

Personalize the "common problem" line based on what you know about them.

5 Social Proof Email Email

"Subject: [Client Name] asked me to reach out

Hi [Name],

[Mutual connection or colleague] mentioned you might benefit from [your service].

We recently helped a [similar business] achieve [specific result] in [timeframe], and [their name] thought you might be facing similar challenges.

Would you be open to a brief call to explore if this could work for you?

No pitch, just a conversation.

Best,
[Your Name]"

The referral mention dramatically increases response rates.

6 Urgency/Limited Time Email Email

"Subject: [Your Service] — 3 spots open this month

Hi [Name],

I'm currently taking on [X] new clients this month and wanted to reach out before my calendar fills up.

I've helped businesses like yours with [specific challenge], and I thought there might be a good fit here.

If you're looking to [solve this problem] in the near future, I'd love to schedule a quick call this week.

If not, no worries — I'll follow up in [timeframe] instead.

Best,
[Your Name]"

Creates urgency without being pushy. Gives them an easy out.

Email Best Practices

Keep subject lines under 50 characters. Lead with a question or curiosity gap. Include a clear but low-pressure CTA. Always sign with your full name and phone number.

Phone Scripts
For warm leads and cold calls

7 Cold Call Opener Phone

"Hi, is this [Name]? Hey, this is [Your Name] with [Your Business]. I hope I'm catching you at a good time.

The reason I'm calling is — I noticed [specific reason] and thought there might be a fit for what we do here.

Can I take 60 seconds to tell you who we help and what we do? [Pause for response]

[After their response] Cool. So we help [target audience] with [problem]. We recently worked with someone in a similar situation and [outcome].

Would it make sense to schedule a quick call to explore this further?"

Keep it under 2 minutes. Ask for the appointment, don't try to sell on the call.

8 Voicemail Script Phone

"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Your Business] at [phone number].

I wanted to reach out because [reason — mutual connection, saw their business, etc.].

It should only take a minute of your time. Give me a call back at [phone number] or feel free to text me.

Again, this is [Your Name] at [number]. Talk soon, thanks!"

Keep voicemails under 30 seconds. Say your number twice. Never leave a "salesy" voicemail.

9 Follow-Up Call Phone

"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name]. I'm following up from the [email/text/interaction] we had [timeframe] ago.

I know you're busy, so I'll be quick — are you still looking to [solve their problem]?

[Listen carefully and respond]

Great! Well, the best next step would be to [specific action]. Would [day/time] work for a quick call? I can send over a calendar link right now."

Always have a specific next step ready before calling.
Objection Handlers
Respond to common pushbacks

10 "I Don't Have Time" Objection

"I totally understand — you're busy. That's actually why this might be perfect. Most of our clients spend [minimal time] on their end and we handle the rest.

Could we do a quick 10-minute call to see if this makes sense for you? If not, no worries at all."

10 "I Need to Think About It" Objection

"Of course, this is a big decision. What specifically are you thinking through? Maybe I can help address that right now.

[After addressing their concern] Is there anything else holding you back? I want to make sure you have everything you need."

10 "Send Me Info" Objection

"Absolutely, I can send over some details. But let me ask — what specifically are you looking to learn more about? That way I can make sure the info I send is most relevant for you.

And just so you know, I have availability [timeframe] — would it make sense to schedule a quick call to go over everything together?"

Never just send info and disappear. Always try to get a next step.

Text & Messaging Scripts

6
SMS/Text Templates
For quick outreach and follow-ups

11 Quick Introduction Text Text

"Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name] from [Business]. Hope you're doing well! I help local businesses with [service] and thought of you.

Would you be open to a quick chat this week? No pressure at all — just thought it might be worth a conversation."

Keep texts under 3 sentences. Always ask a question to get a response.

12 Follow-Up Text Text

"Hey [Name]! Following up on our chat from [timeframe]. Have you had a chance to think about [topic]?

I'm still here if you have any questions. Happy to help either way!"

Wait 3-5 days before following up. Don't be pushy.

13 Google Review Request Text

"Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name] from [Business]. Thanks for being a great client!

If you have 30 seconds, I'd really appreciate a quick review on Google. Here's the link: [YOUR LINK]

It really helps us grow and reach more people. Let me know if you need anything else!"

Include the direct link. Send 30-60 days after working with them.

14 Referral Request Text Text

"Hey [Name]! Hope all is well! I'm looking to grow through referrals right now.

Do you know anyone who could benefit from [service]? I'm offering [incentive] for any referrals that become clients.

Just put my name in and I'll take great care of them!"

Be specific about the type of person you're looking for. Have a referral reward ready.

Text Message Rules

Text between 9am-6pm for best response rates. Never send more than 2 texts without a response. If they don't reply, call or move on.

Social & Local Group Scripts

7
Facebook Groups & Nextdoor
Proven post templates for local visibility

15 Value + Soft CTA Post Social

Topic: [Helpful tip related to your service]

Hey [City/Group Name]! Quick tip about [topic]...

[Share 3-5 actionable pieces of advice]

If anyone has questions about [related topic], feel free to DM me. I help people with [your service] in [area], and I'm always happy to chat.

[Optional: "DM me for a free [lead magnet]"]

Provide real value first. The sell comes through helpfulness, not pushiness.

16 Question/Engagement Post Social

Topic: Community engagement + market research

Hey [City/Group Name]! Quick question for the community...

What's your biggest challenge with [problem your service solves]?

I'm a [your title] and I'm always looking to understand what people in our area need most. Drop your answers below — genuinely curious!

Questions drive engagement. Use responses to fuel follow-up DMs.

17 Client Testimonial Post Social

Topic: Social proof with client permission

Love hearing success stories from our clients! 🙏

[Client Name] recently [specific outcome] and wanted to share their experience:

"[Testimonial quote]"

Ready to get similar results? DM me or check out [link]!

Always get written permission before posting client testimonials.

18 Partnership Outreach Business

"Hi [Business Name],

I run [Your Business] and we serve similar customers. I've been impressed with what you're doing in [specific area].

I'm proposing a referral partnership — when your clients need [your service], I'm your go-to recommendation. In return, I'm happy to refer clients to you when they need [their service].

Worth a 15-minute call to explore this? No pressure if it doesn't make sense for your business.

Best,
[Your Name]"

Keep emails short. Focus on mutual benefit for customers.

19 Door-to-Door Introduction In-Person

"Hi! I'm [Your Name] — I run [Your Business]. We're based right here in [City].

I was hoping to introduce myself and leave you my card. If you ever need [service] or know someone who does, I'd really appreciate the referral.

[Hand card] Thanks for your time!"

Keep under 60 seconds. Smile, make eye contact, and leave quickly. Don't pitch hard on first visit.

20 Past Client Check-In Relationship

"Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name] from [Business]. Hope you've been well since we [worked together/saw each other]!

I was just thinking about you and wanted to say hi. How are things going with [previous project/topic]?

[After exchange] I'm currently taking on a few new clients and wanted to check in. Are you or anyone you know in need of [service]? I'd be happy to offer [discount/referral bonus] for anyone you send my way.

Either way, let me know if there's anything I can help with!"

This is the best source of warm leads. Stay in touch with past clients.

Master Rule: Build Relationships First

The best outreach doesn't feel like outreach. It's helpful. It's genuine. It's building a real connection. When you lead with value and genuine interest in helping, the selling takes care of itself.
