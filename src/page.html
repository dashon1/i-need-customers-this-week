Actions 1-12: Zero-Prep Tasks (Do These First)

01

Post in 5 Local Facebook Groups

Find neighborhood, city, and industry Facebook groups. Post valuable advice + subtle call-to-action.

02

DM 20 Past Customers

Reach out to customers from the last 6 months. Reconnect and ask for referrals.

03

Ask for a Google Review

Text your best customers a direct link to your Google review page. One review can generate 3-5 leads.

04

Post on Nextdoor

Share a helpful tip or local insight in Nextdoor. Include your service offering naturally.

05

Email Your Full Contact List

Send a "we need your help" email to everyone in your database. Ask for referrals.

06

Call 10 Warm Leads

Anyone who has shown interest but hasn't booked. Call, don't text.

07

Partner with a Non-Competitor

Contact 3 businesses that serve the same customers but don't compete with you. Propose a referral swap.

08

Post an Offer on Deal Sites

Craigslist, Groupon clones, local deal aggregators. Limited-time offers work best.

09

Update Your Google Business Profile

Add photos, posts, and current offers. Fresh profiles rank higher.

10

Text Your Best 50 Contacts

Personal text asking how they're doing + "Do you know anyone who needs..."

11

Drop 50 Business Cards

Local coffee shops, libraries, community centers, bulletin boards.

12

Post on LinkedIn (if B2B)

Share industry insight + direct message 10 connections about your service.

Part 3: Proven Scripts & Templates

Section 03

Referral Request Script

✓ The 30-Second Referral Ask

"Hey [Name], I hope you're doing well! I'm actually looking to grow my business through referrals right now — just putting that out there.

Do you know anyone who could benefit from [your service]? It could be a friend, family member, coworker — anyone who's been thinking about [problem you solve].

I'm offering [incentive] for any referrals that turn into clients. And I'll make sure to take great care of anyone you send my way."

Key point: Be specific about the type of person you're looking for. "Anyone who needs help with X" is too vague.
Section 03

Past Customer Reactivation Script

★ Reactivation Text/Email

"Hi [Name]! It's [Your Name] from [Your Business]. Hope you've been well since we worked together [timeframe].

I'm reaching out because I'm currently taking on a few new clients and wanted to check in — are you or anyone you know in need of [service]?

If so, I'd love to offer [special offer] for anyone you refer. And as a past client, I'd also like to offer you [special deal] if it's been a while since we worked together.

Let me know either way — just wanted to say hi!"

Send this to customers from the last 12 months. Time it around holidays or the anniversary of when they first hired you.
Section 03

Warm Lead Follow-Up Call Script

⏱ The Follow-Up Call

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. We [met/connected/spoke] about [topic] a [day/week] ago.

I'm calling to see where you are in your decision process. I know you mentioned [specific concern or interest] — have you had a chance to think about it?

[Listen and address objections]

Great! Would [date] work for us to [next step]? I'll send you a [calendar invite/confirmation/details] right now."

Always have a specific next step ready. Never end the call without booking the next action.

Part 3: More Proven Scripts

7
Section 03

Partnership/Referral Partner Outreach

✓ Business Partnership Email

"Hi [Name],

I run [Your Business] and noticed we're both serving [same type of customer]. I think there's an opportunity for us to help each other grow.

Here's what I'm proposing: When your clients need [your service], I'd love to be your go-to referral. In return, I'm happy to refer my clients to you when they need [their service].

No pressure at all — I just thought it was worth a conversation. Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?"

Keep it simple. Don't overcomplicate the offer. Most business owners are happy to swap referrals if it benefits their customers.
Section 03

Asking for a Google Review

★ Review Request Text

"Hi [Name]! It's [Your Name] from [Business]. Thanks for being an awesome client!

If you have 30 seconds, I'd really appreciate it if you could leave us a quick review on Google. Here's the link: [YOUR GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]

Your review helps other people find us and grow our business. Let me know if you need anything else!"

Include the direct link. The easier you make it, the more reviews you'll get. Aim for 5-10 reviews in a week.
Section 03

Door-to-Door B2B Introduction

✓ In-Person Pitch

"Hi! I'm [Your Name] — I run [Your Business]. We're based right here in [City] and we've been helping local businesses with [service].

I was hoping to introduce myself and leave you my card. If you ever need [service] or know someone who does, I'd really appreciate the referral.

[Hand them your card] Here's my card. Thanks for your time!"

Keep it under 60 seconds. Smile, make eye contact, and leave quickly. Don't try to sell on the first visit.

Part 4: Local Group & DM Templates

Section 04

Local Facebook Group Post Template

Template #1: Value + Soft CTA

Topic: [Helpful tip related to your service]

Hey everyone! I wanted to share a quick tip about [topic]. [Share 3-5 useful pieces of advice].

If anyone has questions about [related topic], feel free to reach out. I help people with [your service] in [area], and I'm always happy to chat.

[Optional: "DM me for a free [lead magnet]"]

Template #2: Question Post

Topic: [Engagement question]

Hey [City] friends! Quick question — what's your biggest challenge with [problem your service solves]?

I'm a [your title] and I'm always looking to understand my community's needs better. Drop your answers below! 🙌

Section 04

Cold DM Scripts

DM Template #1: Compliment + Question

"Hey [Name]! I came across your [profile/post/business] and really liked your approach to [specific thing].

Quick question — are you currently looking for help with [your service]? I work with [type of client] and wondered if this might be relevant for you."

DM Template #2: Value First

"Hey [Name]! I noticed you mentioned [their challenge] in your recent post.

I've helped [similar clients] with this, and one thing that often works is [specific tip].

If you'd like more ideas on this, I'm happy to share. No pressure!"

DM Template #3: Direct but Friendly

"Hey [Name] — I help [target audience] with [your service].

Would you be open to a quick chat if this is something you're working on? Happy to jump on a free 15-min call."

DM Success Rule

Never send a sales pitch in the first DM. Build rapport first. Ask questions. Provide value. The sell comes later — usually after 2-3 exchanges.
